Damage by Part of the Plane:

damage_by_species <- strikes %>%
  select(Species.Name, Radome.Damage, Windshield.Damage, Nose.Damage, Engine1.Damage, Engine2.Damage, Engine3.Damage, Engine4.Damage, Propeller.Damage, Wing.or.Rotor.Damage, Fuselage.Damage, Landing.Gear.Damage, Tail.Damage, Lights.Damage) %>%
  mutate_all(na_if,"") %>%
  drop_na()

damage_by_species <- damage_by_species %>%
  group_by(Species.Name) %>%
summarise(across(everything(), list(sum)))

damage_by_species <- melt(damage_by_species ,  id.vars = 'Species.Name', variable.name = 'Damage')

damage_by_species$value <- as.numeric(damage_by_species$value)

damage_by_species <- damage_by_species %>% 
  group_by(Damage) %>% 
  summarise(value = sum(value))


damage_by_species %>%
  ggplot(aes(value, reorder(Damage, -value), fill=value))+
  geom_bar(width = 0.7, stat = "identity") +

Conclusion:

Number of strikes reported have grown up over time, but we can see fair decrease between 2014 and 2015. Number of accidents increases on July, reaches its peak on August (around 25K) and decreases by December (around 7K).

The most vulnerable parts of the aircraft are Wings, Rotor, and Engine 1 and 2, which mostly get stroke by Birds of Small and Medium size, and also by Mourning Doves and Gulls. Most of the strikes happen when aircraft is either approaching, taking off or landing.

However, the most affected types of aircraft are Boeing-737-300 and Boeing-737-700, Airbus A320, and Bombardier CRJ100/200.

The biggest part of wild strikes happen in Denver International Airport(KDEN), Dallas International Airport(KDWF), and OHare International Airport(KORD).
